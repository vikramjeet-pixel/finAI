From Origin to Legacy: Darwin's Published Works

On the Origin of Species (1859)

Darwin's landmark work presented a meticulously argued case for evolution, providing overwhelming evidence and introducing natural selection as the mechanism. Its publication sparked controversy but transformed science.

Other Major Works

  • The Descent of Man, and Selection in Relation to Sex (1871): Applied his theory to human evolution and introduced the concept of sexual selection.
  • The Expression of the Emotions in Man and Animals (1872): A comparative study on emotional expression.

Modern Legacy

Darwin's theory is the unifying principle of biology and underpins modern fields such as genetics, ecology, and medicine.

Reception and Controversy

Origin of Species was met with fascination and fierce debate. Some scientists praised its evidence; others contested its implications for human origins and theology. Over time, the weight of data won out.

Influence on Modern Science

  • Genetics: Mendelian inheritance and later molecular biology integrated with selection.
  • Ecology: Population dynamics and adaptation studies trace back to Darwinian ideas.
  • Medicine: Pathogen evolution informs vaccines and antibiotic stewardship.
