Charles Darwin: The Man Who Redefined Life

Charles Darwin (1809–1882) was a pioneering naturalist and biologist whose revolutionary theory of evolution by natural selection transformed our understanding of life on Earth. His work laid the foundation for modern biology and continues to shape scientific thought today.

The Core Idea

Darwin's theory proposes that all living things share a common ancestor and change gradually over time. These changes, known as evolution, are driven by natural selection—where the environment determines which traits are most advantageous for survival and reproduction.

Biography Highlights

  • Early Life: Born in Shrewsbury, England; studied at Edinburgh and Cambridge.
  • Influences: Inspired by geologist Charles Lyell and naturalist John Henslow.
  • Down House: Conducted extensive experiments and writing throughout his life.
  • Legacy: Transformed biology with evolution by natural selection.
