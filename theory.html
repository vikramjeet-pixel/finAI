Survival of the Fittest: Explaining Natural Selection

Background

Evolution means change over time. Darwin provided the mechanism: natural selection. Alfred Russel Wallace independently conceived a similar idea, which spurred Darwin to publish his work.

The Four Pillars of Natural Selection

  1. Variation: Individuals in a species are all slightly different.
  2. Inheritance: Traits are passed from parent to offspring.
  3. High Rate of Population Growth: More offspring are produced than can survive, leading to a struggle for existence.
  4. Differential Survival and Reproduction: Those with advantageous traits are more likely to survive and reproduce, passing those traits on.

Defining 'Fitness'

In evolutionary terms, "fitness" means reproductive success, not physical strength. The fittest are those who leave the most viable offspring.

Evidence Supporting Evolution

  • Fossil record: Transitional forms document gradual change over time.
  • Comparative anatomy: Homologous structures indicate shared ancestry.
  • Biogeography: Species distributions align with evolutionary history.
  • Genetics: DNA comparisons quantify relatedness among species.

Common Misconceptions

  • Evolution is not a ladder of progress; it is branching change.
  • Natural selection does not "plan"; it favors existing beneficial variations.
  • Individuals do not evolve; populations change over generations.
